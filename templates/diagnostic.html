Diagnostic Analysis

Diagnostic analysis answers "Why did it happen?" by digging deeper into the patterns behind fraud. We use our trained XGBoost model's feature importances and statistical comparisons to understand what separates fraudulent transactions from legitimate ones.

XGBoost Feature Importance

Values are XGBoost gain-based importances from the deployed pipeline in model.joblib: each bar is that encoded column's share of total gain (roughly 1,400 post-encoding columns sum to 100%). The strongest individual inputs are amount (~5.4%), amount deviation from the customer's mean (~3.6%), and hour (~2.1%). Individual category levels also rank highly; summed over all levels, high-cardinality fields job (~38%) and merchant (~31%) contribute heavily, plus category (~12%) overall—matching the preprocessors in modelling/preprocessing.py.

Transaction Amounts: Fraud vs. Legitimate

Figures come from the full cleaned dataset (~3.48M rows). The average fraud transaction is $538 compared with about $69 for legitimate ones. Medians are $437 (fraud) vs $44 (legit). The fourth bar uses the 99th percentile (~$486 legit vs ~$1,170 fraud) so the chart is not skewed by extreme legitimate outliers (max legit ~$53k in the data).

Key Diagnostic Findings

High-Amount Transactions

Fraudsters tend to make large purchases to maximize value before a card is cancelled. Transactions above $400 are significantly more likely to be fraudulent than those under $100.

Unusual Spending for the Customer

The pipeline uses amt_dev_from_mean (amt - cust_amt_mean from modelling/features.py). Large positive deviations align with higher fraud risk in the model.

Late-Night Activity

Transactions between 10 PM and 3 AM are 10x to 25x more likely to be fraudulent than daytime transactions. Cardholders are typically asleep and unable to detect and respond to unauthorized use.

Online vs. In-Person

Online categories (shopping_net, misc_net) show fraud rates 3x to 12x higher than in-person categories. The lack of physical card verification makes card-not-present fraud far easier for criminals.
