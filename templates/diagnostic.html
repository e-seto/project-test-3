Diagnostic Analysis

Diagnostic analysis answers "Why did it happen?" by digging deeper into the patterns behind fraud. We use our trained XGBoost model's feature importances and statistical comparisons to understand what separates fraudulent transactions from legitimate ones.

XGBoost Feature Importance

The transaction amount is by far the most important predictor of fraud, accounting for 40.5% of the model's decision-making power. Combined with amount deviation (how far the transaction is from a customer's average), spending behavior explains over 65% of fraud detection. The hour of day is the third most important feature at 11.8%, confirming the late-night fraud pattern we saw in the descriptive analysis.

Transaction Amounts: Fraud vs. Legitimate

Fraudulent transactions have a dramatically different spending profile. The average fraud transaction is $538 compared to just $69 for legitimate ones. Half of all fraud transactions exceed $437, while half of legitimate transactions are under $44. This gap is why amount-based features dominate the model.

Key Diagnostic Findings

High-Amount Transactions

Fraudsters tend to make large purchases to maximize value before a card is cancelled. Transactions above $400 are significantly more likely to be fraudulent than those under $100.

Unusual Spending for the Customer

When a transaction deviates far from a customer's typical spending pattern, the risk of fraud increases sharply. A purchase 5x above a customer's average is a strong red flag.

Late-Night Activity

Transactions between 10 PM and 3 AM are 10–25x more likely to be fraudulent than daytime transactions. Cardholders are typically asleep and unable to detect unauthorized use.

Online vs. In-Person

Online categories (shopping_net, misc_net) show fraud rates 3–12x higher than in-person categories. The lack of physical card verification makes card-not-present fraud far easier for criminals.
