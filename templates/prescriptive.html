Prescriptive Analysis

Prescriptive analysis answers "What should we do about it?" Based on our model's performance, we designed a three-tier decision policy that balances fraud prevention with customer experience.

Three-Tier Decision Policy

Our model outputs a fraud probability between 0% and 100% for each transaction. We use two thresholds to create three decision zones. These thresholds were tuned to maximize fraud detection while keeping false positives manageable.

Approve (below 60%)

Transactions with a fraud probability below 60% are automatically approved. This covers the vast majority of legitimate transactions. Keeping the approve cutoff at 60% fraud probability limits false declines, as a wrongly blocked purchase frustrates cardholders and costs the merchant revenue.

Manual Review (60% up to <85%)

Transactions with probability at least 60% but still below 85% are flagged for human review (exactly 85% is treated as a block). These are suspicious but not certain fraud as a customer may be travelling or making an unusual purchase. A fraud analyst reviews the details and contacts the cardholder if needed. This middle zone protects both the customer and the institution.

Block (85% or higher)

Transactions scoring 85% or higher are automatically blocked. At this confidence level, the model is highly certain the transaction is fraudulent. The card issuer declines the charge and may freeze the card, notifying the cardholder immediately to verify their identity.

Practical Recommendations for Financial Institutions

Enhanced Monitoring at Night

Deploy stricter thresholds for transactions between 10 PM and 3 AM, when fraud rates are 10x to 25x higher. Consider requiring additional authentication (SMS code, push notification) for high-value late-night purchases.

Online Transaction Safeguards

Since online categories show 3x to 12x higher fraud rates, implement additional verification for card-not-present transactions, such as 3D Secure (Verified by Visa, Mastercard SecureCode) and device fingerprinting.

Customer Spending Profiles

Maintain real-time spending profiles per cardholder. Flag transactions that deviate significantly from a customer's typical amount as our model shows this is the second most important fraud signal after the raw transaction amount.

Threshold Tuning

The review cutoff (60%) and block cutoff (85%) should be periodically re-tuned as fraud patterns evolve. Institutions with lower fraud tolerance can tighten thresholds, while those prioritizing customer experience can loosen them.
